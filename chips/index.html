8th Grade Civics Simulation

Chelsea Wheatley v. Chips Yoho Cookie Co.

Interactive Classroom Script • ~25 Minute Performance

Highlight Your Character's Lines:

⚖️ Case Information & Legal Issue

Case Name: Chelsea Wheatley v. Chips Yoho Cookie Company

Plaintiff's Claim: Consumer Fraud (NJ Consumer Fraud Act, Statute 56:8-2)

Harm Alleged: Hospitalization of minor Noah Wheatley due to wheat ingredients.

Burden of Proof: Clear & Convincing Evidence (for Consumer Fraud)

Main Legal Question: Was the "glutton-free" symbol misleading enough to deceive a reasonable consumer, or does responsibility rest on the buyer to read the ingredients?

THE COURTROOM SCRIPT

Stage directions are noted in grey, italic text brackets.

Scene 1: Court is in Session
🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE (Judge Harold T. Justice)

*(Bangs the gavel three times)*

Order in the court! All rise for the Honorable Judge Harold T. Justice. This court is now in session. Today, we hear the case of Chelsea Wheatley versus the Chips Yoho Cookie Company. The Plaintiff is suing for consumer fraud, alleging deceptive product packaging under the New Jersey Consumer Fraud Act, Statute 56:8-2. They are seeking medical expenses and the removal of the "glutton-free" logo. The defense claims the packaging is clear, truthful, and that the consumer bears the responsibility of checking ingredients.

*(The Judge pauses and addresses the jury directly)*

Members of the jury—our remaining students here today—it is your job to listen carefully to the evidence. To win, the Plaintiff must prove by a preponderance of the evidence that the defendant's packaging was an unconscionable deception. However, for a finding of legal consumer fraud, the standard is clear and convincing evidence. Keep that in mind as we begin. Counselor Proctor, please present your opening statement.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

*(Stands up, walks to the center of the courtroom, and addresses the jury)*

Thank you, Your Honor. Ladies and gentlemen of the jury, imagine sending your child to a safe kitchen cabinet, only to have them end up in an emergency room hours later, violently ill and severely dehydrated. That is the nightmare my client, Chelsea Wheatley, lived through on December 4, 2025. Her twelve-year-old son, Noah, has celiac disease—a serious genetic autoimmune disorder where eating even a microscopic speck of gluten, a protein found in wheat, causes his body to attack its own digestive system.

*(Holds up the mock cookie box, displaying the front "GF" logo)*

Ms. Wheatley did what any caring mother would do. She looked for a gluten-free label. She saw this box. She saw a bold, square logo with a giant "GF" on the front. Any reasonable consumer in a hurry would recognize that as the universal symbol for "Gluten-Free." But it didn't say "Gluten-Free." It said "Glutton-Free"—a sneaky, dangerous play on words designed to exploit busy parents. Chips Yoho claims this is just a clever marketing joke. But to a sick child, it is not a joke. It is fraud. We will prove that this packaging is an unconscionable deception, and we ask that you hold this company accountable. Thank you.

*(Sits back down at the plaintiff's table)*

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Thank you, Counselor. Counselor Defensor, your opening statement.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

*(Stands up confidently, walks toward the jury)*

Thank you, Your Honor, and thank you, members of the jury. This case is not about a lack of sympathy for a sick child. We all feel terrible that young Noah got sick. But this case is about the law, facts, and personal responsibility. My client, Charlie Ownerman, is an entrepreneur who wanted to make healthy cookies that people could enjoy without guilt—so healthy, you could be a glutton and eat more than one! Thus, the slogan: "Free to be a Glutton."

*(Points to the cookie box)*

The packaging is entirely literal. It says "Glutton-Free," and indeed, eating these cookies won't make you a glutton. But more importantly, the law is clear: food manufacturers must list their ingredients. Chips Yoho did exactly that. On the back, in bold letters, it clearly states: "Contains Wheat." It was placed in the regular health food aisle, not the specialty gluten-free aisle. Ms. Wheatley failed to read the ingredient list of a food she was giving to a child with a life-threatening medical condition. You cannot sue a company because you didn't read the label. We ask you to return a verdict for the defense. Thank you.

*(Sits down)*

Scene 2: The Plaintiff's Case
🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Counselor Proctor, call your first witness.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

The Plaintiff calls Chelsea Wheatley to the stand.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

*(As Chelsea Wheatley walks to the stand, places hand on imaginary book)*

Do you swear to tell the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth, so help you God?

👩 CHELSEA WHEATLEY (Plaintiff)

I do. *(Sits down in witness chair)*

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

Ms. Wheatley, please state your name and explain to the court why we are here today.

👩 CHELSEA WHEATLEY (Plaintiff)

My name is Chelsea Wheatley. I am here because my son, Noah, was hospitalized on December 4, 2025, due to the deceptive and highly dangerous packaging on Chips Yoho cookies.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

Tell us about Noah's medical condition.

👩 CHELSEA WHEATLEY (Plaintiff)

Noah was diagnosed with celiac disease in August 2023. He had suffered for months with severe stomach pain. The doctors told us his intestines were badly damaged and it would take six months of a strict, zero-tolerance gluten-free diet to heal. Since that diagnosis, we have been incredibly careful. No wheat, no barley, no rye. He hadn't had a single drop of gluten in over two years.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

Take us to the afternoon of December 4th, 2025.

👩 CHELSEA WHEATLEY (Plaintiff)

It was National Cookie Day, and I wanted to buy Noah a special treat. I ran to ShopLeft during my lunch break. I was rushed because I had to get back to the office. I scanned the shelves in a hurry and saw this new brand, Chips Yoho. Right on the front was a large, square logo with the letters "GF" inside. It looked exactly like the certified gluten-free logos I see on all his other safe snacks. I bought them, rushed home, put them in Noah's designated "safe snack cabinet," and went back to work for an evening meeting.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

What happened next?

👩 CHELSEA WHEATLEY (Plaintiff)

*(Becoming emotional)*

I got a call on my cell phone from Noah. He was crying, and I could hear him violently vomiting in the background. He had eaten half the box, assuming they were safe because they were in his cabinet. I rushed home, found him pale and shaking, and drove him straight to the emergency room. He was admitted overnight and put on an IV because he was dangerously dehydrated.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

*(Shows the cookie box to the witness)*

Ms. Wheatley, does this "glutton-free" logo look misleading to you?

👩 CHELSEA WHEATLEY (Plaintiff)

Absolutely! When you are a busy parent juggling work and grocery shopping, you look for visual cues. A square "GF" logo means "Gluten-Free" to anyone in the allergy community. Substituting "glutton" for "gluten" is a trap. It is incredibly misleading and highly dangerous.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

Thank you, Ms. Wheatley. Your witness, Counselor.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Counselor Defensor, you may cross-examine the witness.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

*(Stands and slowly approaches Ms. Wheatley)*

Ms. Wheatley, I am very sorry to hear about Noah's hospitalization. However, as a parent of a child with a severe, life-threatening food allergy, is it your standard practice to buy random food brands without reading the back of the package?

👩 CHELSEA WHEATLEY (Plaintiff)

I usually check very carefully. But the "GF" symbol on the front was so prominent that I trusted it.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

But you didn't actually read the back, did you?

👩 CHELSEA WHEATLEY (Plaintiff)

I was in a rush on my lunch break—

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

A simple "yes" or "no" will suffice, Ms. Wheatley. Did you read the ingredient list on the back of the box before purchasing?

👩 CHELSEA WHEATLEY (Plaintiff)

No, I did not.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

If you had turned the box around, you would have seen the words "Contains Wheat" printed in bold, black letters. Correct?

👩 CHELSEA WHEATLEY (Plaintiff)

Yes, but—

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

And you would agree that wheat contains gluten?

👩 CHELSEA WHEATLEY (Plaintiff)

Yes, obviously.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

So, if you had spent just five seconds reading the back of the package, your son would not have gotten sick. Is that correct?

👩 CHELSEA WHEATLEY (Plaintiff)

The front of the package lied to me! It designed a trap using the letters "GF"!

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

The package did not lie, Ms. Wheatley. It said "Glutton-Free." G-L-U-T-T-O-N. Which means "excessive eating." It did not say "Gluten." No further questions, Your Honor.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Thank you, Ms. Wheatley. You may step down. Counselor Proctor, call your next witness.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

The Plaintiff calls Mark Etman.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

*(As Mark Etman is sworn in)*

Please state your name and occupation for the record.

🔬 MARK ETMAN (Witness for Plaintiff)

My name is Mark Etman. I am 25 years old, and I have worked as a professional marketing designer for the past four years. I also have celiac disease, which I was diagnosed with at age 15.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

Mr. Etman, why are you testifying today?

🔬 MARK ETMAN (Witness for Plaintiff)

Because the exact same thing happened to me. On November 27, 2024, I purchased a box of Chips Yoho cookies. Like Ms. Wheatley, I saw the giant "GF" square on the front of the box. As a marketing professional, I can tell you that the design is a deliberate copy of the certified gluten-free logo used by the Gluten-Free Certification Organization.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

In your professional opinion as a marketing expert, what is the impact of this design?

🔬 MARK ETMAN (Witness for Plaintiff)

In marketing, we study "visual hierarchy." Consumers scan packages in less than two seconds. A square seal with a stylized "GF" is a universally recognized shorthand for "safe from gluten." By using "GF" to stand for "Glutton-Free" in almost the identical font and shape, Chips Yoho created a massive risk of consumer confusion. It's a classic bait-and-switch.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

What happened when you ate these cookies?

🔬 MARK ETMAN (Witness for Plaintiff)

I ate three of them. Within an hour, I was severely sick. I couldn't stop vomiting and ended up having to call 911. I spent the night in the hospital on an IV for dehydration.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

Is there any doubt in your mind that this packaging caused your injury?

🔬 MARK ETMAN (Witness for Plaintiff)

None whatsoever. The packaging is an unconscionable marketing gimmick that prioritizes "cleverness" over human safety. It needs to be banned.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

Thank you, Mr. Etman. Your witness.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Counselor Defensor, you may cross-examine.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

Mr. Etman, you are a professional marketer, correct?

🔬 MARK ETMAN (Witness for Plaintiff)

Yes, I am.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

So you know that companies use brand slogans all the time. What is Chips Yoho’s slogan, printed right on the front of the box?

🔬 MARK ETMAN (Witness for Plaintiff)

It says, "Free to be a Glutton."

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

"Free to be a Glutton." It does not say "Free of Gluten." In fact, is there any place on this entire box where the word "gluten" is spelled G-L-U-T-E-N?

🔬 MARK ETMAN (Witness for Plaintiff)

No.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

As an adult living with a severe autoimmune disease for ten years, do you make it a habit to trust front-of-package marketing without reading the legally mandated allergen warnings on the back?

🔬 MARK ETMAN (Witness for Plaintiff)

When a package has a certified-looking "GF" symbol, yes, because that symbol is supposed to mean the product has been tested.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

But this symbol didn't say "Certified Gluten-Free." It said "Glutton-Free." You assumed, and your assumption got you sick. Isn't that the truth?

🔬 MARK ETMAN (Witness for Plaintiff)

I assumed based on a highly deceptive visual design that was engineered to make me make that assumption.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

No further questions, Your Honor.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Thank you, Mr. Etman. You may step down. Counselor Proctor, does the Plaintiff rest?

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

The Plaintiff rests, Your Honor.

Scene 3: The Defense's Case
🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Counselor Defensor, you may call your first witness.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

The Defense calls Charlie Ownerman, the creator of Chips Yoho.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

*(As Charlie Ownerman is sworn in)*

State your name and occupation for the record.

🍪 CHARLIE OWNERMAN (Defendant)

I'm Charlie Ownerman, owner and creator of the Chips Yoho Cookie Company.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

Mr. Ownerman, please tell the jury why you created this cookie brand.

🍪 CHARLIE OWNERMAN (Defendant)

Look, growing up, I struggled with my weight. I loved sweets, but I always felt so guilty eating them. As an adult, I wanted to create a cookie that was healthy—low calorie, low sugar—but still tasted amazing. I wanted to tell my customers, "Hey, these are so healthy, you can eat a bunch of them and not feel guilty!" You are free to be a glutton!

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

And is that where the "Glutton-Free" logo came from?

🍪 CHARLIE OWNERMAN (Defendant)

Exactly! "Glutton-Free" means you are free from the guilt of gluttony. It was a lighthearted, fun play on words to reinforce our healthy recipe. It was never, ever intended to trick people with celiac disease. I am a small business owner, not a monster.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

Where did you place the allergen warnings on your product?

🍪 CHARLIE OWNERMAN (Defendant)

Right where they belong by FDA law. On the back, directly underneath the ingredients, in bold capital letters, it says "CONTAINS WHEAT."

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

Where do you distribute these cookies, and where are they shelved in grocery stores?

🍪 CHARLIE OWNERMAN (Defendant)

I personally deliver them to stores like ShopLeft. I explicitly instruct the managers never to put them in the specialty gluten-free aisle. They are shelved in the general "Health Food" section because they are low-sugar, not gluten-free. We have never advertised ourselves as gluten-free.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

Thank you, Mr. Ownerman. Your witness, Counselor.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Counselor Proctor, you may cross-examine.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

*(Stands up and walks over with the cookie box)*

Mr. Ownerman, you claim this was a "lighthearted play on words." Do you know what font is used for the certified medical "Gluten-Free" logo?

🍪 CHARLIE OWNERMAN (Defendant)

I don't know, some basic sans-serif font.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

It is a bold, black Arial font inside a perfect square. Your "Glutton-Free" logo uses the exact same bold font, the exact same square border, and the same abbreviation: "GF." Are you telling this jury that it was a pure coincidence that your logo looks virtually identical to the medical "Gluten-Free" logo?

🍪 CHARLIE OWNERMAN (Defendant)

"GF" stands for "Glutton-Free"! It's our brand logo!

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

But you knew that people with celiac disease actively search for "GF" logos, didn't you?

🍪 CHARLIE OWNERMAN (Defendant)

I knew that they look for gluten-free products, yes. But I expected them to read the back of the package like any responsible person with a medical condition would do.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

If a consumer is in a rush, is it not highly predictable that they would mistake your "GF" logo for a medical "Gluten-Free" logo?

🍪 CHARLIE OWNERMAN (Defendant)

We can't control how fast people shop. The information is right there on the package.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

But you designed the front of the package to send a completely different message than the back, didn't you?

🍪 CHARLIE OWNERMAN (Defendant)

No, the front says "Free to be a Glutton." That's a different word entirely!

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

A word that is one letter off from "gluten." No further questions, Your Honor.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Thank you, Mr. Ownerman. You may step down. Counselor Defensor, call your next witness.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

The Defense calls Katie Storegirl.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

*(As Katie Storegirl is sworn in)*

Please state your name and occupation.

🏪 KATIE STOREGIRL (Witness for Defense)

My name is Katie Storegirl. I am the general manager of the ShopLeft food store.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

Ms. Storegirl, do you recall the afternoon of December 4, 2025?

🏪 KATIE STOREGIRL (Witness for Defense)

Yes, I do. It was busy because of National Cookie Day. I remember Ms. Wheatley checking out at our registers.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

Where were the Chips Yoho cookies located in your store on that day?

🏪 KATIE STOREGIRL (Witness for Defense)

They were located in Aisle 4, which is our "Health Food and Organic" aisle.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

Do you have a separate aisle specifically for gluten-free products?

🏪 KATIE STOREGIRL (Witness for Defense)

Yes, Aisle 6 is our designated "Gluten-Free Specialty" aisle. We keep all certified gluten-free cookies, breads, and pastas there.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

Were Chips Yoho cookies ever stocked in Aisle 6?

🏪 KATIE STOREGIRL (Witness for Defense)

Never. Mr. Ownerman specifically requested they be placed in the general health food aisle because they contain wheat. We followed those instructions.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

In your experience running a grocery store, whose job is it to make sure a product is safe for a customer's specific dietary allergies?

🏪 KATIE STOREGIRL (Witness for Defense)

It’s always the customer’s responsibility. Store staff are always available to help answer questions, and we have clear signage. But we cannot read the labels for the customers. They have to look at what they are buying, especially if it’s a matter of health.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

Thank you, Ms. Storegirl. Your witness.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Counselor Proctor, cross-examination.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

Ms. Storegirl, is Aisle 4, the "Health Food and Organic" aisle, popular with shoppers who have dietary restrictions?

🏪 KATIE STOREGIRL (Witness for Defense)

Yes, it is.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

Do many products in Aisle 4—the organic and health aisle—happen to be gluten-free?

🏪 KATIE STOREGIRL (Witness for Defense)

Some of them are, yes.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

So it is highly plausible that a customer shopping in Aisle 4 might assume a product with a big "GF" logo on it is indeed gluten-free?

🏪 KATIE STOREGIRL (Witness for Defense)

They might assume it, but they would still be wrong if they didn't check the label.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

But you'd agree that placing a product with a "GF" logo in a "Health Food" aisle creates a highly confusing environment for a rushed shopper?

🏪 KATIE STOREGIRL (Witness for Defense)

We put health products in the health aisle. Chips Yoho is low-calorie, so it belongs there. We can't police what logos companies put on their boxes.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

No further questions, Your Honor.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Thank you, Ms. Storegirl. You may step down. Counselor Defensor, does the defense rest?

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

The Defense rests, Your Honor.

Scene 4: Closing Arguments & Jury Charge
🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

We will now hear closing arguments. Counselor Proctor, you have the floor.

👔 PLAINTIFF'S ATTORNEY (Pat Proctor)

*(Addresses the jury)*

Members of the jury, the Consumer Fraud Act was written to protect citizens from "unconscionable commercial practices, deceptions, and misrepresentations." It does not require the company to commit a crime; it only requires that they create a deceptive, misleading environment.

Chips Yoho chose to use a bold, square logo with the letters "GF" in the exact visual style of a certified medical gluten-free logo. They chose to use a word—"Glutton"—that is a single vowel away from "Gluten." They placed this confusing package in a health food aisle. This wasn't a harmless joke; it was a calculated risk that put corporate profits over human safety. A mother trusted what she saw, and a child paid the price in a hospital bed. Do not let this company hide behind fine print on the back of a box. Find Chips Yoho guilty of consumer fraud. Thank you.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Thank you. Counselor Defensor, your closing.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Devin Defensor)

*(Addresses the jury)*

Ladies and gentlemen, this is a tragic case of a genuine mistake—but it was a mistake made by the consumer, not the manufacturer. Chips Yoho was completely transparent. They printed "Free to be a Glutton" on the front. They printed "Contains Wheat" in bold letters on the back. They never stocked their product in the gluten-free aisle.

If we hold companies liable when consumers refuse to read basic ingredient labels, we set a dangerous precedent. Food safety relies on a partnership: companies must list allergens, and consumers must read them. Ms. Wheatley failed her end of that partnership. Do not punish a small business for a consumer's oversight. Return a verdict for the defense. Thank you.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

*(Bangs gavel once)*

Thank you, counsel. Members of the Jury, you have heard the evidence. Now, it is your turn to perform your civic duty. To find the defendant, Chips Yoho, liable for consumer fraud, the Plaintiff must prove by clear and convincing evidence that the packaging was an unconscionable deception, misrepresentation, or misleading practice that caused harm.

You will now retire to the jury room—your classroom—to deliberate. You must elect a Jury Foreperson to lead your discussion. Once you have reached a decision, you will present your verdict to the court. This court is recessed. *(Bangs gavel)*

🗣️ Juror Instructions & Deliberation Prompts

Now, the class acts as the jury. Discuss these four key points to decide your verdict:

  1. The Logo: Was the "glutton-free" logo an unconscionable act of deception?
  2. The Buyer's Duty: Is a parent of a child with severe food allergies legally responsible for checking the ingredients, regardless of the front marketing?
  3. Visual Design: Was the logo too visually similar to the certified medical "GF" logo, causing unfair confusion?
  4. Shelf Placement: Did the product's placement in the health food aisle (rather than the gluten-free aisle) clarify or worsen the confusion?

Essential Question:

How do we decide what is for the common good when people disagree?

Today, we will step back in time to 1970. The U.S. government has a massive plan: to dam the Delaware River. This project promises progress, but at a great cost. Your task is to debate whether the Tocks Island Dam should be built.

Key Concepts

Common Good

The benefit or interests of all. Decisions made for the common good are meant to help everyone in a community, not just a small group.

Civic Virtue

The dedication of citizens to the common welfare of their community, even at the cost of their individual interests. It's about being an active, responsible participant in society.

Eminent Domain

The right of a government to take private property for public use, with payment of compensation.

The Outcome

The Plan (1962)

A 37-mile lake for flood control, water supply, and recreation.

The Reality (Today)

The project was cancelled in 1975, creating the Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area.

Citizen action and changing priorities led to the preservation of the river valley.

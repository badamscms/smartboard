The Tocks Island Dam Debate

Essential Question:

How do we decide what is for the common good when people disagree?

Today, we will step back in time to 1970. The U.S. government has a massive plan: to dam the Delaware River. This project promises progress, but at a great cost. Your task is to debate whether the Tocks Island Dam should be built.

Timeline of Events

1955: Hurricane Diane

A massive hurricane causes the worst flood in the Delaware River's recorded history, leading to widespread destruction and calls for better flood control.

1962: Project Authorized

The U.S. Congress authorizes the Army Corps of Engineers to build the Tocks Island Dam for flood control, water supply, and hydroelectric power.

1965-1970s: Land Acquisition & Protests

The government begins buying thousands of acres of private land, forcing families to move. Local residents and a growing environmental movement begin to organize protests against the dam.

1975: The Project is Halted

Facing immense public opposition, environmental concerns, and rising costs, the governors of the surrounding states vote to de-authorize the project. The dam is never built.

The Lay of the Land

This map shows the Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area. The proposed dam would have been built near the southern end, flooding the river valley northward for 37 miles, creating a massive lake where you now see a winding river.

Debate Guide

Rules of Engagement

  • One person speaks at a time.
  • Listen respectfully to opposing views.
  • Attack the idea, not the person.
  • Use evidence from the reading.
  • Be prepared to explain your reasoning.

Teacher's Discussion Prompts

  • How do you justify your sacrifice for the 'common good'?
  • Is it possible to achieve your goals without harming others?
  • What is the long-term value of your position?
  • Whose vision of the future is better for the most people?

Key Concepts

Common Good

The benefit or interests of all. Decisions made for the common good are meant to help everyone in a community, not just a small group.

Civic Virtue

The dedication of citizens to the common welfare of their community, even at the cost of their individual interests. It's about being an active, responsible participant in society.

Eminent Domain

The right of a government to take private property for public use, with payment of compensation.

The Outcome

The Plan (1962)

A 37-mile lake for flood control, water supply, and recreation.

The Reality (Today)

The project was cancelled in 1975, creating the Delaware Water Gap National Recreation Area.

Citizen action and changing priorities led to the preservation of the river valley.

⚖️ Case Information & Legal Issue

Case Name: State of New Jersey v. Maria Smith (Goodman Township)

Charge: Unlawful Possession of a Handgun (N.J.S.A. 2C:39-5b)

Date of Incident: March 4, 2026 (Approximately 2:00 AM)

Burden of Proof: Beyond a Reasonable Doubt (Criminal Standard)

Key Legal Defense: The Defense of Necessity / Justification (Protecting human life against an active home intruder)

THE COURTROOM SCRIPT

Stage directions are noted in grey, italic text brackets.

Scene 1: Court is in Session & Opening Statements
🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE (Judge Sylvia Vance)

*(Bangs the gavel three times)*

Order in the court! All rise for the Honorable Judge Sylvia Vance. This court is now in session. Today, we hear the criminal matter of The State of New Jersey versus Maria Smith. The Defendant stands charged with Unlawful Possession of a Handgun under New Jersey Statute 2C:39-5b, a third-degree crime.

The State alleges that Ms. Smith knowingly possessed and discharged a firearm without a valid permit issued in her name. The defense argues that her possession was temporary, completely justified by an immediate threat of death or serious bodily harm to herself and her sleeping children.

*(The Judge addresses the student jury)*

Members of the jury—our students who represent the citizens of Goodman Township—it is your solemn duty to evaluate the evidence. Because this is a criminal trial, the burden rests entirely on the State. To convict, the prosecution must prove every element of the crime beyond a reasonable doubt. If they fail, you must acquit. Counselor Arthur, please present the State’s opening statement.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

*(Stands up, steps to the podium, and addresses the jury)*

Thank you, Your Honor. Ladies and gentlemen of the jury, New Jersey has some of the safest and strictest firearm laws in the nation. These laws exist to keep firearms out of the hands of unlicensed individuals. We are not here to question Ms. Smith’s fears on the night of March 4, 2026. We are here because she made a sequence of highly reckless, unlawful choices that endangered an entire neighborhood.

The facts are undisputed: Ms. Smith did not possess a license to carry or own a firearm. In the middle of the night, she unlocked her husband’s closet, took a registered weapon, and deliberately loaded it. She didn't stop there. She fired two live rounds into her ceiling, rushed out onto her front porch, and waved that handgun in the air, shouting threats into the dark, public street. In doing so, she became a threat to the community. We will show that her possession of the firearm went far beyond self-defense, and we ask that you uphold the law. Thank you.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Thank you, Counselor. Ms. Defensor, your opening statement.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

*(Stands up, approaches the jury with a warm but firm tone)*

Thank you, Your Honor, and thank you, jurors. Imagine waking up at two o'clock in the morning. Your husband is miles away on a business trip. Your two young children are asleep in the next room. Suddenly, downstairs, you hear a door slam shut, and heavy footsteps begin to march up your stairs. You have seconds to act. What do you do?

Ms. Smith did what any loving mother would do to protect her children from a predator. She accessed a securely locked, legally registered firearm belonging to her husband. She didn't seek out a weapon; she sought out a shield. She fired warning shots to scare off a criminal, and she successfully defended her family.

The law recognizes a powerful defense called **"necessity."** It states that a person is justified in breaking a technical law if it is the only way to avoid an immediate, life-threatening catastrophe. Ms. Smith's actions were necessary, safe, and fully justified. We ask you to find her not guilty. Thank you.

Scene 2: The Prosecution's Case
🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Counselor Arthur, call your first witness.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

The State calls Officer Robert Brown to the stand.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

*(As Officer Brown walks to the stand and raises his right hand)*

Do you swear to tell the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth, so help you God?

👮 OFFICER ROBERT BROWN (Witness for Prosecution)

I do. *(Sits down in the witness stand)*

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

Officer Brown, please state your name, profession, and take us to the early hours of March 4, 2026.

👮 OFFICER ROBERT BROWN (Witness for Prosecution)

My name is Officer Robert Brown, and I have been with the Goodman Township Police Department for eight years. Just past 2:00 a.m. on March 4th, dispatch received an emergency call from a resident, Jennifer Williams, who reported hearing two loud gunshots. She stated that her neighbor across the street was outside, waving a handgun in an extremely erratic manner.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

What did you find when you arrived at the scene?

👮 OFFICER ROBERT BROWN (Witness for Prosecution)

My partner and I arrived at the residential neighborhood. My partner secured Ms. Williams' house, while I went to Mrs. Smith's residence. Mrs. Smith opened the door and let me in. She was visibly shaken. I questioned her about the shots fired. She stated that she heard a burglar inside her home and retrieved a gun to defend her family.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

Did you ask to see her firearms permit?

👮 OFFICER ROBERT BROWN (Witness for Prosecution)

Yes, I did. Under New Jersey law, anyone possessing or carrying a handgun must have a valid state-issued purchaser identification card and a carry permit in their name. When I requested these documents, Mrs. Smith admitted that she did not have a license. She told me the gun belonged strictly to her husband, who was away on a business trip.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

Based on that admission, what action did you take?

👮 OFFICER ROBERT BROWN (Witness for Prosecution)

I took the firearm into evidence and placed Ms. Smith under arrest for unlawful possession of a handgun. The law is clear: a firearms permit is personal and non-transferable. You cannot inherit or borrow a permit just because you are married.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

Thank you, Officer. Your witness, Counselor.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Counselor Defensor, you may cross-examine.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

*(Stands and walks toward Officer Brown)*

Officer Brown, did you inspect Mrs. Smith's home for signs of a break-in?

👮 OFFICER ROBERT BROWN (Witness for Prosecution)

We did a sweep. We found a kitchen door in the rear of the house that was unlocked and slightly open, consistent with her account of a forced entry.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

And you have no reason to doubt that an actual burglar entered her home while her children slept upstairs, correct?

👮 OFFICER ROBERT BROWN (Witness for Prosecution)

The entry was consistent with a burglary attempt, yes.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

If Ms. Smith had not fired those warning shots, is it possible the burglar would have reached her children's bedroom?

👮 OFFICER ROBERT BROWN (Witness for Prosecution)

I cannot speculate on what might have occurred, counselor. My job was to address the unlicensed weapon.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

But when you arrived, Ms. Smith did not point the gun at you, did she? She was cooperative and let you into her home immediately, correct?

👮 OFFICER ROBERT BROWN (Witness for Prosecution)

Yes, she was cooperative inside the home.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

Thank you. No further questions, Your Honor.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Thank you, Officer Brown. You may step down. Counselor Arthur, call your next witness.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

The State calls Jennifer Karen Williams.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

*(As Ms. Williams is sworn in)*

Please state your name and occupation for the court.

👵 JENNIFER KAREN WILLIAMS (Witness for Prosecution)

My name is Jennifer Williams. I am retired, and I have lived in Goodman Township for 42 years.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

Ms. Williams, take us to the night of March 4th. What woke you up?

👵 JENNIFER KAREN WILLIAMS (Witness for Prosecution)

I was fast asleep when two incredibly loud gunshots startled me out of bed. I was terrified! I got up, ran to my front window, and looked across the street at the Smiths' house. I saw a dark figure run out their door. A few seconds later, Ms. Smith came stumbling out onto her porch. She had a gun in her hand.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

How was she handling the gun when she was outside?

👵 JENNIFER KAREN WILLIAMS (Witness for Prosecution)

Carelessly! Incredibly carelessly! She was waving it around, pointing it down the street, screaming threats. She seemed completely erratic, highly excited, and ready to shoot again. If that gun had gone off on the street, the bullets could have gone straight through my bedroom window or hit another neighbor's house! There was absolutely no safety discipline whatsoever.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

What did you do next?

👵 JENNIFER KAREN WILLIAMS (Witness for Prosecution)

I backed away from the window and dialed 911. We live in a quiet, safe community. No one should be allowed to run out into a public street waving a deadly weapon without a license. It was extremely dangerous.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

Thank you, Ms. Williams. Your witness.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Counselor Defensor, you may cross-examine.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

*(Approaches the witness stand)*

Ms. Williams, you mentioned in your statement that you have "always avoided the Smiths" because you "thought they were strange people." Is that correct?

👵 JENNIFER KAREN WILLIAMS (Witness for Prosecution)

Well... yes. They keep to themselves. They are a bit odd.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

So, you had a pre-existing bias against Ms. Smith before this incident even happened, didn't you?

👵 JENNIFER KAREN WILLIAMS (Witness for Prosecution)

Certainly not! A gun is a gun. My feelings about them have nothing to do with her waving a firearm in my face at 2 a.m.!

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

Let’s discuss that porch. It was pitch black outside, was it not?

👵 JENNIFER KAREN WILLIAMS (Witness for Prosecution)

Yes, it was night.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

You were looking through a glass window pane, across a wide two-lane street, in the dark, without your glasses?

👵 JENNIFER KAREN WILLIAMS (Witness for Prosecution)

I could see quite clearly! The streetlights were on.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

Is it possible Ms. Smith was simply pointing the firearm toward the floor of her porch to keep her distance from the escaping burglar, rather than "carelessly waving it at your house"?

👵 JENNIFER KAREN WILLIAMS (Witness for Prosecution)

She looked very dangerous to me. I felt threatened.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

Thank you. No further questions.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Ms. Williams, you may step down. Counselor Arthur, does the State rest?

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

The State rests, Your Honor.

Scene 3: The Defense's Case
🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Counselor Defensor, you may call your first witness.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

The defense calls Maria Smith to the stand.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

*(As Maria Smith walks to the stand and places her hand on the book)*

Do you swear to tell the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth, so help you God?

👩 MARIA SMITH (Defendant)

I do. *(Sits down)*

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

Mrs. Smith, please describe what happened in your home on March 4, 2026.

👩 MARIA SMITH (Defendant)

I woke up around 2:00 a.m. to use the bathroom. As I stepped back into the hallway, I heard the heavy wood of our back kitchen door opening, slamming shut, and then footsteps down in our kitchen. My husband, John, was hours away in Philadelphia on a business trip. My two small kids were fast asleep in the bedroom right next to mine. I was completely alone with an intruder in the dark.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

What did you do?

👩 MARIA SMITH (Defendant)

I panicked, but then I heard footsteps climbing our wooden stairs. They were coming up to where my kids were sleeping! I ran into our bedroom closet, opened my husband’s gun safe, and grabbed his registered pistol. I loaded the weapon, opened the door, and stood in the hallway. I yelled multiple times: "I have a gun! Get out of my house!" But the footsteps kept coming.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

How did you make the decision to fire?

👩 MARIA SMITH (Defendant)

I knew I couldn't shoot a person unless my life was in absolute, immediate danger, and I didn't want to hurt anyone. So, as a final warning, I pointed the barrel straight up at the ceiling and fired two shots. The noise was deafening. Thankfully, it worked. I heard the intruder scramble back down the stairs and run out the front door.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

Why did you follow the intruder outside?

👩 MARIA SMITH (Defendant)

I wanted to make sure they were completely gone and weren't waiting on the porch. I was still terrified, checking the dark yard. I held the gun securely because I didn't know if he was still hiding. I never waved it around recklessly! I kept my finger off the trigger and held it pointed down. I went back inside after five minutes and called the police.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

Do you feel your arrest is just?

👩 MARIA SMITH (Defendant)

Absolutely not! It is a travesty. I didn't ask to be terrorized in my own home. I used my husband's firearm as a last resort to protect my innocent children. What was I supposed to do? Wait for an intruder to enter my children's bedroom?

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

Thank you, Mrs. Smith. Your witness, Counselor.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Counselor Arthur, you may cross-examine the witness.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

*(Stands up confidently)*

Mrs. Smith, you knew that New Jersey law strictly prohibits carrying or possessing a firearm without an individual license, correct?

👩 MARIA SMITH (Defendant)

Yes, I know the general rules. But this was an emergency.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

But you and your husband had active discussions about getting you a license over several years, didn't you?

👩 MARIA SMITH (Defendant)

Yes, we did.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

And you decided against it because of the "complexity and paperwork"?

👩 MARIA SMITH (Defendant)

Yes, the process is extremely long and difficult in New Jersey.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

So, you knowingly chose to bypass the legal background checks and licensing procedures that every other lawful citizen must complete?

👩 MARIA SMITH (Defendant)

I didn't think I would need to defend myself from a burglar in the middle of the night!

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

Once the intruder fled the house and ran down the street, the immediate threat to your life inside your home was gone, correct?

👩 MARIA SMITH (Defendant)

I didn't know if he was gone or waiting on the porch.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

But you still stepped out of your safe, locked home and stood on a public street with an unlicensed firearm in your hand. You did not call the police first; you took the weapon outside first. Is that correct?

👩 MARIA SMITH (Defendant)

Yes, to make sure the threat was gone.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

No further questions, Your Honor.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Thank you, Mrs. Smith. You may step down. Counselor Defensor, call your next witness.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

The defense calls David Johnson to the stand.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

*(As David Johnson is sworn in)*

Please state your name and occupation for the record.

🎯 DAVID JOHNSON (Witness for Defense)

My name is David Johnson. I have worked as a firearms instructor at the Goodman Township Shooting Range for 21 years.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

Do you know the defendant, Maria Smith?

🎯 DAVID JOHNSON (Witness for Defense)

Yes, I do. Both Maria and her husband John have been regular clients at my range for several years. I’ve personally given Maria dozens of hours of instruction on handling firearms, loading, operating safety switches, and understanding gun safety laws.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

Based on your professional instruction, how would you evaluate Ms. Smith's capability with a handgun?

🎯 DAVID JOHNSON (Witness for Defense)

Maria is an incredibly safe, careful, and competent shooter. She is actually better trained than many people who hold standard firearm licenses. She understands mechanical safeties, recoil control, and bullet trajectory. She knows that firing into a ceiling is a way to scare off an intruder without causing physical harm to neighbors, as the structure absorbs the impact.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

Do you see any danger in her utilizing her husband's firearm in this emergency?

🎯 DAVID JOHNSON (Witness for Defense)

None at all. In an emergency of life or death, a tool is a tool. She was trained, she knew how to use it safely, and her actions saved her life. Punishing her for using the only tool available to save her kids is nonsensical.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

Thank you, Mr. Johnson. Your witness.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Counselor Arthur, cross-examination.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

Mr. Johnson, you are a certified instructor. Does any part of New Jersey's state guidelines recommend that an unlicensed civilian fire "warning shots" into a ceiling inside a densely populated residential zone?

🎯 DAVID JOHNSON (Witness for Defense)

In a standard scenario, no. But this wasn't a standard scenario. This was a home invasion.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

A warning shot is a discharge of a weapon. Once a bullet is fired, even into a ceiling, there is a risk of ricochet, structural penetration, or injury to someone upstairs. Correct?

🎯 DAVID JOHNSON (Witness for Defense)

There's a microscopic risk. But that is vastly lower than the risk of letting a burglar climb into her children's bedroom.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

But as an expert, you would agree that New Jersey law does not allow one spouse to "share" a gun license under any normal circumstance. Correct?

🎯 DAVID JOHNSON (Witness for Defense)

Yes, that is the technical law.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

No further questions.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Thank you, Mr. Johnson. You may step down. Counselor Defensor, does the defense rest?

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

The defense rests, Your Honor.

Scene 4: Closing Arguments & Jury Charge
🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

We will now hear closing arguments. Counselor Arthur, you may proceed.

👔 STATE PROSECUTOR (Arthur Vance)

*(Addresses the jury)*

Members of the jury, this case is not about whether Mrs. Smith is a good person. It is about whether she broke a vital safety law. She knowingly avoided getting a permit for years because she found the paperwork "too complex." On March 4th, she chose to load and possess a firearm she had no legal right to handle.

Even if you believe she was initially justified in grabbing the gun inside, her actions afterward are completely inexcusable. Once the burglar fled, she stepped out onto a public porch in a residential area, waving a handgun in the air, yelling into the night. She didn't call the police from the safety of her home; she brought a dangerous weapon into a public space. Firearm regulations exist to prevent exactly this kind of chaotic, reckless behavior. To protect our community, you must hold her accountable. Find the defendant guilty. Thank you.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

Thank you, Counselor. Ms. Defensor, your closing argument.

💼 DEFENSE'S ATTORNEY (Danielle Defensor)

*(Addresses the jury)*

Members of the jury, the prosecution wants you to think this is a technicality about paperwork. They want to punish a mother for choosing her children’s lives over a gun permit. The law is not a suicide pact. The New Jersey defense of **necessity** is clear: you are allowed to break a minor law if doing so is the *only* way to prevent a much more devastating tragedy.

An intruder was climbing her stairs. If she had not grabbed that gun and fired those warning shots, what would have happened to her children? She handled the firearm safely, scared the predator away, and stood on her porch for a few short minutes to make sure her children were secure. She was not reckless; she was brave. Find her not guilty, and send a message that a mother has the right to defend her family. Thank you.

🧑‍⚖️ THE JUDGE

*(Bangs gavel once)*

Thank you, counsel. Members of the Jury, you have heard all the testimony. Under New Jersey Criminal Law, to find the defendant guilty, the State must prove beyond a reasonable doubt that Maria Smith knowingly possessed a handgun without a permit.

However, you must also evaluate the defense of **Necessity (Justification)**. If you find that Ms. Smith faced an imminent, life-threatening situation, that her choice to possess the gun was the only reasonable way to prevent a greater harm, and that she did not create the danger herself, then you must find her **Not Guilty**.

Please retire to your classroom, discuss the evidence, elect a Jury Foreperson, and return with a unanimous verdict of either **Guilty** or **Not Guilty**. This court is now in recess. *(Bangs gavel)*

🗣️ Juror Instructions & Deliberation Prompts

Now, the class acts as the jury. To find Maria Smith guilty, the prosecution must prove the charge beyond a reasonable doubt, and you must reject the "necessity" defense. Discuss these five critical sub-issues:

  1. The Danger: Were Ms. Smith's actions fully justified by the imminent, threatening presence of an active home intruder?
  2. The Warning Shots: Was firing two warning shots into her ceiling a safe and necessary defense tactic, or did it recklessly endanger her sleeping children and neighborhood?
  3. The Front Porch: Once the burglar fled, did Ms. Smith's choice to stand on her porch and wave the gun cross the line from "necessity" into "unlawful possession"?
  4. The Permit Laws: Does a spouse have an implied right to access a legally registered firearm in a crisis, or must gun laws be applied absolute and strictly?
  5. The "What If": What other realistic choices did Ms. Smith have on March 4th, and did she choose the least harmful option?
